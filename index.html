Niche Integral

$${ \int \frac{ \left[ x^{x^x} \cdot x^x \left( \ln x(1+\ln x) + \frac{1}{x} \right) \cdot \Gamma(\operatorname{Si}(x)) \cdot \operatorname{W}_0(\phi^x) + x^{x^x} \cdot \Gamma(\operatorname{Si}(x)) \psi^{(0)}(\operatorname{Si}(x)) \frac{\sin x \operatorname{W}_0(\phi^x)}{x} + \frac{x^{x^x} \Gamma(\operatorname{Si}(x)) \operatorname{W}_0(\phi^x) \ln \phi}{1 + \operatorname{W}_0(\phi^x)} \right] \cdot \left( \operatorname{erf}(\sqrt{x}) + \operatorname{Li}_2\left(\frac{1}{1+x^2}\right) \right) - \left( x^{x^x} \Gamma(\operatorname{Si}(x)) \operatorname{W}_0(\phi^x) \right) \cdot \left( \frac{e^{-x}}{\sqrt{\pi x}} + \frac{2x \ln\left( \frac{x^2}{1+x^2} \right)}{1+x^2} \right) }{ \left( \operatorname{erf}(\sqrt{x}) + \operatorname{Li}_2\left(\frac{1}{1+x^2}\right) \right)^2 } \, dx }$$

At first glance, this integral appears to be a chaotic collection of transcendental functions. However, the structure is a precisely engineered application of the Quotient Rule in reverse.

The fundamental identity being used is:

$$\int \frac{u'(x)v(x) - u(x)v'(x)}{[v(x)]^2} \, dx = \frac{u(x)}{v(x)} + C$$

Step 1: Identify the Parent Functions

We define the numerator function $u(x)$ and the denominator function $v(x)$ from the integrand:

Numerator: $$u(x) = x^{x^x} \cdot \Gamma(\operatorname{Si}(x)) \cdot \operatorname{W}_0(\phi^x)$$
Denominator: $$v(x) = \operatorname{erf}(\sqrt{x}) + \operatorname{Li}_2\left(\frac{1}{1+x^2}\right)$$

Step 2: Differentiate the Numerator \(u'(x)\)

Applying the Product Rule to $u(x)$ requires three separate derivatives:

  • Tower of Power: $\frac{d}{dx}(x^{x^x}) = x^{x^x} \cdot x^x \left( \ln x(1+\ln x) + \frac{1}{x} \right)$
  • Gamma of Sine Integral: $\frac{d}{dx}\Gamma(\operatorname{Si}(x)) = \Gamma(\operatorname{Si}(x)) \psi^{(0)}(\operatorname{Si}(x)) \frac{\sin x}{x}$
  • Lambert W Function: $\frac{d}{dx}\operatorname{W}_0(\phi^x) = \frac{\operatorname{W}_0(\phi^x) \ln \phi}{1 + \operatorname{W}_0(\phi^x)}$

Combining these yields the massive bracketed expression in the first half of the integral's numerator.

Step 3: Differentiate the Denominator \(v'(x)\)

Differentiating $v(x)$ involves the Error Function and the Dilogarithm:

  • Error Function: $\frac{d}{dx}\operatorname{erf}(\sqrt{x}) = \frac{e^{-x}}{\sqrt{\pi x}}$
  • Dilogarithm: $\frac{d}{dx}\operatorname{Li}_2\left(\frac{1}{1+x^2}\right) = \frac{2x \ln\left( \frac{x^2}{1+x^2} \right)}{1+x^2}$

This matches the subtracted term in the second half of the integral's numerator.

Final Result

Since the integrand is exactly $\frac{d}{dx} [u(x)/v(x)]$, the solution is simply the ratio of our two parent functions:

$$\frac{ x^{x^x} \cdot \Gamma(\operatorname{Si}(x)) \cdot \operatorname{W}_0(\phi^x) }{ \operatorname{erf}(\sqrt{x}) + \operatorname{Li}_2\left(\frac{1}{1+x^2}\right) } + C$$
